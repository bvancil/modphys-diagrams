\begin{frame}{Measuring events}
\Large
\structure{How do we measure events?}
\begin{columns}[t]
\begin{column}<2->{.45\linewidth}
\header{\underline{time}}
\begin{itemize}
\item<4-> clock reading \visible<9->{($t$)}
\item<5-> time interval \visible<9->{($\Delta t$)}
\end{itemize}
\end{column}
\begin{column}<3->{.45\linewidth}
\header{\underline{length}}
\begin{itemize}
\item<6-> position \visible<9->{($x$)}
\item<7-> displacement \visible<9->{($\Delta x$)}
\item<8-> distance \visible<9->{($d$)}
\end{itemize}
\end{column}
\end{columns}
\structure{Discussion}
\begin{itemize}
\item<10-> Try to design a situation in which ``clock reading'' and ``time interval'' have different values.  Draw a picture.
\item<11-> Try to design a situation in which ``position'', ``displacement'', and ``distance'' have different values.  Draw a picture.
\item<12-> What does ``\header{instant}'' mean?
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\end{tikzpicture}

\begin{column}{.4\linewidth}\large
To make a connection between our everyday experience and meters per second, it is useful to know that 10 mph is the same as 4.4704 meters per second.  A car traveling at 20 meters per second is traveling about 45 mph.  The acceleration of the other car was chosen to be 2.5 meters per second per second.
\end{column}
