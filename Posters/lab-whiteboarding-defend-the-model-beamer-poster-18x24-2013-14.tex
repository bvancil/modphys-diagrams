%  1. A “defend the mathematical model” protocol for whiteboarding labs. Students would have to argue things like that the model makes reasonable preditions for (a) large values of the IV and (b) small values of the IV, (c) that the scaling (as you mentioned above) makes sense, and that (d) the error bars wouldn’t accommodate a simpler or alternate model, all in order to justify the fit. (I had trouble with (d) this year when students would argue for a linear model when a constant one was more reasonable.) Think of these as conceptual tools for the R of CER.

  \begin{document}
  \begin{frame}{Defend the Model} 
  \vspace{-3cm}
    \vfill\normalsize
        \begin{exampleblock}{Example}
        		Let's say you did a sampling of various cars and found that gas mileage $G$ (in mpg) decreases as the mass of a car $m$ (in pounds) increases.  You fit a line (your \emph{mathematical model}) to your data and find:
			\begin{equation*}
				G = -0.0043\text{ mpg/pound}\cdot m + 60\text{ mpg}
			\end{equation*}
        \end{exampleblock}
    \vfill
    \begin{block}{Conceptual tools}
      \begin{rulelist}
        \item Does my mathematical model make reasonable predictions for \punchit{large values} of the independent variable? \\
%        \begin{exampleblock}{Example}
%        		You might notice that the graph of your mathematical model $G(m)$ decreases as a constant rate.  At some mass it will be equal to 0~mpg, which means that a car with this mass would go zero miles for every gallon of gas it burns.  At \emph{any} higher mass, the gas mileage will be negative, meaning that a car with this higher mass would go \emph{negative} miles(?!) for every gallon of gas it burns.  What does this mean?
%        \end{exampleblock}

        \item Does my mathematical model make reasonable predictions for \punchit{small values} of the independent variable?
        \item Does the type of my mathematical model (e.g. constant, linear, inversely proportional) have reasonable \punchit{scaling behavior} (e.g. when I change the horizontal variable by a factor of $n$, what happens to the vertical variable?)?
        \item Could the \punchit{uncertainty} in my measurements be consistent with a simpler or alternate mathematical model?
      \end{rulelist}
    \end{block}
    \vfill

  \end{frame}
